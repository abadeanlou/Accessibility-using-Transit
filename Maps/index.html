How far can a city take you?

Interactive maps of public-transport accessibility for three European cities — Torino, Milano, and Paris — computed from open GTFS schedules: each city's transit network is rebuilt from its timetables, travel times are routed over a hexagonal grid (with OSRM walking legs), and every cell is colored by how well transit serves it. Methodology from my first-author papers at hEART 2022 and TRB 2023.

Map types — P2P: average travel time from each cell to the whole city · P2POI: travel time to points of interest · POI2P: the catchment view — who can reach each amenity · Reachable POIs: how many amenities are reachable within time thresholds. Maps are self-contained exports of several MB — give them a few seconds.
